Judging a Book by its Title...

A data visualization story by Jack Manning


We all love fantasy books. Right? The profound characters, rich worldbuilding, and a seemingly endless supply of creativity. Or maybe not. This idea was inspired by a segment on a gameshow they cleverly titled "A title of words" where contestants were supplied a word bank of nouns and confronted with the trope of titles blank of blank. Like Game of Thrones or Lord of the Rings or Well of Ascension (for all my Brandon Sanderson fans). After the segment had finished and everyone moved on I couldn't help but let my mind wander, just how many titles were like this. As I combed through my own personal library the dots started to connect, more tropes emerging from the shelves. With this knowledge of title trends and the desire to uncover more oddities in my favorite passtime I set out on a quest to answer the question, is it ok to judge a book by it's title?

I embarked on this journey with the help of the Goodreads API, a wonderful source of all things nerdy books that provided a list of books and their information on the scale that I had hoped. I was able to collect roughly 15,000 Fantasy and Sci-Fi titles to do analysis on, and after some extensive cleaning and data organization I had a dataset rich with interesting trends and unique quirks.

A Title of Words

Blank of Blank books

This trope is the largest group of titles I could find, including 18.8% of all the Fantasy and Sci-Fi titles that were included in the dataset.

Not only did it have the largest quantity it also has some of the best quality books, according to popularity. The two most famous fantasy fandoms, Game of Thrones and Lord of the Rings, both fall into this trope. And their new TV shows, House of Dragons and Rings of Power respectively, also fall victim to this common title structure.

Other notable inclusions are...

  • Life of Pi
  • Way of Kings
  • Throne of Glass
  • The Chronicles of Narnia
  • Name of the Wind

Blank and Blank books

Word

One Word titles

This is the second largest category of books, coming in with 2,636 book titles, 18.2% of the overall dataset. This trope was far larger than I had expected as there are only so many words that an author can use to entice a reader.

Despite this there are some extremely well-known titles in this category, including...

  • Dune
  • Twilight
  • Divergent
  • Watchmen
  • 1984

The name

The One Word books

This is the companion trope to one word titles, including enough individual books to be worth a mention as a part of this study. Similar to blank and blank titles it only takes up about 5% of the overall dataset, but that is over 620 titles.

While the category is small the titles it includes have large franchises. Books in this category include...

  • The Giver
  • The Odyssey
  • The Martian
  • The Lorax
  • The Stand
