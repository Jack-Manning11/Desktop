Judging a Book by it's Title...

A data visualization story by Jack Manning

We all know the adage "Don't judge a book by it's cover" but nobody has said anything about judging one by it's title. I am a self proclaimed lover of all things Sci-Fi and Fantasy, but with that comes a nerdy obsession for the details, and I found an interesting connection when browsing through some Fantasy series. I started to realize that an awful lot of Fantasy titles were following some predictable behaviors, and I wanted to see how widespread it was, so I begain this quest. Let's peruse the shelves of Fantasy novels and try to solve the mystery of title creativity.

In my analysis I found quite a few categories that contained enough books to be worth talking about. Each of these categories are mutually exclusive (no book is a part of two categories) and the analysis is done on around 15,000 of the top rated Sci-Fi and Fantasy books according to the GoodReads API. The categories are as follows, Blank of Blank, Blank and Blank, One word, "The" One Word, and The Adjective Noun.
